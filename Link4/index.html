Net Migration in 2012 by Country

Net migration refers to the difference between the number of people entering a country or region (immigrants) and the number of people leaving (emigrants) over a specific period, typically a year. It essentially measures the net gain or loss of population due to migration. Data Sources: Countries from Natural Earth 50M scale data; Population and Net Migration data from the World Bank, World Development Indicators.
